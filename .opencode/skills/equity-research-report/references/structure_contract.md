# Structure Contract

Document organization rules for institutional research reports.

## Complete Document Structure

```
1. Cover Page
2. Table of Contents
3. Executive Summary
4. Body Section 1: [Topic Overview]
   4.1. Subsection 1.1
   4.2. Subsection 1.2
5. Body Section 2: [Analysis]
   5.1. Subsection 2.1
   5.2. Subsection 2.2
6. Body Section 3: [Valuation/Recommendation]
   6.1. Subsection 3.1
   6.2. Subsection 3.2
7. Body Section 4: [Risk Factors]
   7.1. Subsection 4.1
   7.2. Subsection 4.2
8. Appendix: Where to Find Stuff
9. Glossary of Terms
10. Disclosure Appendix
```

## Section Specifications

### 1. Cover Page

**Layout:** Two-column (60% / 40%)

**Left Column (60%):**
- Investment summary (3-4 bullet points)
- Key financials table (Revenue, EBITDA, EPS, P/E, etc.)
- Target price and recommendation
- Price chart or performance graph

**Right Column (40%):**
- Analyst information box (light gray background)
- "N Things to Know" numbered list (5-7 items)
- Company snapshot (Market cap, Sector, Exchange, Ticker)

**Bottom:**
- Full regulatory disclaimer (8pt text)

**Content Density:** HIGH - pack information tightly

### 2. Table of Contents

**Format:**
```
Section                                    Page
─────────────────────────────────────────────
Executive Summary                          3
1. Company Overview                        4
   1.1 Business Description                4
   1.2 Industry Position                   5
2. Investment Thesis                       6
   2.1 Growth Drivers                      6
   2.2 Competitive Advantages              7
3. Financial Analysis                      8
   3.1 Revenue Breakdown                   8
   3.2 Profitability Metrics               9
4. Valuation                               10
   4.1 DCF Analysis                       10
   4.2 Comparable Company Analysis         11
5. Risk Factors                            12
Appendix: Where to Find Stuff             13
Glossary of Terms                         14
Disclosure Appendix                       15
```

**Styling:**
- Section titles: Left-aligned
- Page numbers: Right-aligned
- Dotted leaders between title and page number
- Indent subsections (1.1, 1.2 under 1.0)

### 3. Executive Summary

**Opening Paragraph:** FULLY BOLD (entire paragraph)

Content:
- Core investment thesis (1-2 sentences)
- Target price and recommendation
- Key catalysts
- Risk/reward summary

**Length:** 200-300 words

**Format:**
```
**[Company Name] (NSE: TICKER | Bloomberg: TICKER IN) is a [sector] company
that [core thesis]. We initiate/start/upgrade coverage with a [rating]
recommendation and [currency] [target price] target price, representing
[upside/downside]% upside/downside from current levels.**

[Supporting paragraphs in regular weight]
```

### 4. Body Sections

**Minimum sections:** 4-5 major sections

**Each section must contain:**
- Section heading (H1): 16-18pt, bold, underlined
- Multiple subsections (H2): 12-13pt, bold
- Multiple paragraphs per subsection (minimum 2)
- At least one exhibit (chart or table) per section
- Analysis supported by data and exhibits

**Typical sections for equity research:**

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Company Overview | Business description, history, management, strategy |
| Industry Analysis | Market size, growth, trends, competitive landscape |
| Investment Thesis | Growth drivers, catalysts, competitive advantages |
| Financial Analysis | Revenue, margins, cash flow, balance sheet |
| Valuation | DCF, comparables, sum-of-parts, target price derivation |
| Risk Factors | Key risks, mitigation, sensitivity analysis |

### 5. Exhibits

**Numbering:** Sequential across entire document (Exhibit 1, Exhibit 2, etc.)

**Types:**
- Charts (bar, line, pie, waterfall)
- Tables (financial data, comparables, sensitivity)
- Diagrams (process flow, organizational structure)

**Placement:**
- Appear close to first reference in text
- Never split across pages if possible
- Source line required below every exhibit

**Format:**
```
Exhibit [N]: [Descriptive Title]
[Chart or Table]
Source: [Source Name], [Date]
```

### 6. Appendix: Where to Find Stuff

**Purpose:** Quick reference for data sources and methodology

**Content:**
- Data sources used in the report
- Methodology explanations
- Additional data tables
- Historical data references

**Format:**
```
Appendix: Where to Find Stuff
─────────────────────────────

Data Sources:
• Financial data: [Source]
• Market data: [Source]
• Industry data: [Source]

Methodology:
• Valuation approach: [Description]
• Forecasting method: [Description]
• Risk assessment: [Description]
```

### 7. Glossary of Terms

**Purpose:** Define key terminology for readers

**Format:**
```
Glossary of Terms
─────────────────

Beta: A measure of a stock's volatility relative to the overall market.
     A beta of 1.0 indicates the stock moves in line with the market.

EBITDA: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization.
        A measure of a company's operating performance.

P/E Ratio: Price-to-Earnings Ratio. The current share price divided by
           earnings per share.
```

**Styling:**
- Term: Bold, 10pt
- Definition: Regular, 10pt
- Alphabetical order preferred

### 8. Disclosure Appendix

**Required Elements:**

1. **Analyst Certification**
   ```
   The research analyst(s) whose name(s) appear(s) on this report certify that:
   (1) All views expressed in this report accurately reflect their personal views
   about the subject company and its securities; and
   (2) No part of their compensation was, is, or will be directly or indirectly
   related to the specific recommendations or views expressed in this report.
   ```

2. **Rating Definitions**
   ```
   Rating          Definition
   ─────────────────────────────────────
   Buy             Expected to outperform by >15%
   Hold            Expected to perform within ±15%
   Sell            Expected to underperform by >15%
   ```

3. **Risk Rating**
   ```
   Low Risk        < 15% annualized volatility
   Medium Risk     15-25% annualized volatility
   High Risk       > 25% annualized volatility
   ```

4. **Company-Specific Disclosures**
   - Ownership interest
   - Investment banking relationships
   - Other material conflicts

5. **General Disclosures**
   - Market-making activities
   - Rating distribution
   - Important notices

6. **Contact Information**
   - Firm address
   - Phone numbers
   - Email addresses
   - Website

## Paragraph Rules

### Minimum Requirements
- **Paragraph length:** 100-1,000 words
- **Subsection requirement:** Every subsection must have MORE than ONE paragraph
- **Transitions:** Natural language transitions (avoid "First, second, third")

### Good Transitions
- "Building on this analysis..."
- "In contrast to the previous section..."
- "Another key consideration..."
- "Beyond the financial metrics..."
- "From a risk perspective..."

### Bad Transitions
- "First, second, third"
- "Firstly, secondly, lastly"
- "1. First point. 2. Second point."

## Exhibit Rules

### Numbering
- Sequential: Exhibit 1, Exhibit 2, Exhibit 3...
- Do NOT reset numbering in new sections
- Reference in text as "Exhibit [N]"

### Source Citations
- Every exhibit must have a source line
- Format: "Source: [Source Name], [Date]"
- Style: 8pt, italic, gray
- Position: Below exhibit, left-aligned

### Table Headers
- Background: #D6DCE4 (light blue-gray)
- Text: Bold, black
- **NOT** dark navy with white text

## Document Length

| Report Type | Target Pages |
|-------------|--------------|
| Company Initiation | 35-50 pages |
| Update Report | 15-25 pages |
| Sector Report | 40-60 pages |
| Strategy Report | 30-45 pages |
| Credit Report | 25-40 pages |

## Quality Standards

### Must Have
- [ ] Cover page with navy banner
- [ ] Dense content (not minimalist)
- [ ] Two-column cover layout
- [ ] Table of Contents with page numbers
- [ ] Bold executive summary opening
- [ ] Sequential exhibit numbering
- [ ] Source citations on all exhibits
- [ ] Light blue-gray table headers
- [ ] Headers and footers
- [ ] Glossary of Terms
- [ ] Disclosure Appendix
- [ ] 35+ pages (for full reports)

### Must Not Have
- [ ] Colored callout boxes
- [ ] Minimalist cover
- [ ] Dark navy table headers
- [ ] Missing source citations
- [ ] Numbered transitions (First, second, third)
- [ ] Exhibits without "Exhibit N:" prefix
- [ ] Missing glossary or disclosures
